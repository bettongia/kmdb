# v0.10.01

## Purpose

This is a **hardening track**, following the precedent set by
[0_02_01](completed/0_02_01.md) — the roadmap that closed out the durability
findings of the 2026-05-22 code review. It exists to work through the findings
of the **2026-07-18 release-readiness review**
([docs/reviews/release-readiness-review-2026-07-18.md](../reviews/release-readiness-review-2026-07-18.md)),
which recommended **against tagging `0.1.0`** in the codebase's current state.

> ### ⚠️ Callback to the review — do not close this track early
>
> **The review is the authoritative status board for this work, and it is not
> yet complete.** Two of its eight workstreams were never executed, and the
> review says so explicitly in its §9 "Coverage actually achieved":
>
> - **W1 — spec conformance.** 34 spec files / 10,728 lines were **not**
>   systematically traced to code and tests. This is the single largest gap.
> - **W4 — concurrency and durability.** The 0.02.01 regression check,
>   `FaultyStorageAdapter` coverage, and **O-4** (the vault indexing isolate vs
>   §18's synchronous model) are all outstanding.
>
> Additionally, W5 (CLI) and W6 (code health) were only **partially** completed
> — see §9 for the specific commands and checks that were never exercised.
>
> **Every item in the review must be worked through before `0.1.0` is tagged,
> not merely the criticals addressed by the first plan below.** When the
> Group A/B/C work lands, **return to the review**, complete W1 and W4, and
> then walk §6's findings register end to end. The review's §10 groups all
> findings by shared remediation work and is the intended starting point.

## Threat-model decision (Group C) ✅ Decided

The review's **E-1** established that `docs/spec/31_encryption.md` documents a
**passive-only** threat model — a provider who *reads* your data — and never
claims the synced data is authentic. Five of the six critical findings follow
from that single gap.

**Decision (2026-07-19): close the gap before `0.1.0`, scoped to T1.**

- **T1 (untrusted provider, read *and* write) — closed in this track.** All sync
  artefacts are authenticated. The provider cannot forge SSTables, vault blobs,
  leases, or high-water marks. The realistic threat this defeats is **account
  compromise** — a phished Google account or stolen OAuth token yields write
  access to the sync folder, and neither full-disk encryption nor the provider's
  own at-rest encryption helps there.
- **T3 (malicious peer) — designed, deferred.** A compromised peer already holds
  the DEK and full plaintext, so authentication buys attribution and blast-radius
  containment, not confidentiality. It needs per-device asymmetric identity,
  enrollment, and revocation. Captured as a proposal, not built here.

**Authentication is decoupled from encryption.** Encryption stays optional and is
sold on its own merits. The reasoning: with full-disk encryption and a reputable
provider, KMDB-level encryption is genuinely marginal for many users — so
forcing it as a *prerequisite* for authentication would trade a low-value
requirement for a high-value guarantee. The sync-auth key is also a
fundamentally lower-stakes secret than the DEK: losing it means re-provisioning,
not losing your data, so it needs no passphrase and no recovery code.

## Work items

### Sync trust boundary and authentication (Groups A + B + C)

Originally one plan; **split into three on 2026-07-19** after the
`kmdb-plan-reviewer` pass found it too large to hold at `Investigated` (nine
phases, three packages, an external repo, a new subsystem, a new user-facing
UX, and a breaking format change). The three are independent and may proceed in
parallel.

| Plan | Scope | Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| [plan_0_10_01_sync_trust_boundary.md](../plans/plan_0_10_01_sync_trust_boundary.md) | **Validation & robustness** — parser hardening (S-1, S-3), decompression bounds (S-2), vault integrity (S-4), indexing-isolate lifecycle (D-1), lease validation and staging (S-6, S-7), the hostile-input test corpus | **Investigated** |
| [plan_0_10_01_value_aad.md](../plans/plan_0_10_01_value_aad.md) | **E-2** — bind ciphertext to its namespace/key/type. Breaking format change; wants to land before `0.1.0` freezes the format | Open |
| [plan_0_10_01_sync_authentication.md](../plans/plan_0_10_01_sync_authentication.md) | **T1** — `SecretStore`, `SyncAuthenticator`, transport envelope, device enrollment | Open |

**Sequencing note.** The hardening plan is worth doing **regardless of how the
threat model lands** — corruption, buggy peers, and malformed PDFs exist without
any attacker — so it is not blocked on the other two. AAD is independent of the
secret store and could land first. Sync authentication is the largest and least
specified.

> **D-1 was added to the hardening plan after the fact** (Phase 3b), from the W4
> workstream: a dead vault-indexing isolate hangs `close()` *before* the memtable
> flush. It sits there rather than in Group E because S-2 and S-8 supply its
> trigger.

> **R-5 — a consequence worth surfacing early.** Once artefacts are
> authenticated, everything already in an existing sync root becomes
> unverifiable. The operation is **"wipe the sync root and re-push from one
> device"**, not "re-run `remote add`". Needs to reach the release notes.

### Release dependency gate (Group D)

The review's **O-1** confirmed the `betto_*` closure is **12 packages**, all
still at `-dev`. Blocking work, but mechanical:

- Publish all 12 at a suffix-free `0.1.0`.
- Add **`betto_abnf`** to `CLAUDE.md`'s package list and the workspace
  `dependency_overrides` — it reaches core `kmdb` transitively via `betto_schema`
  and is currently undocumented (O-1).
- Reconcile the `kmdb_flutter` / `kmdb_icloud` pins, which sit a dev release
  behind `kmdb` and resolve independently (O-1b).
- Confirm `kmdb_flutter` is covered by a CI lane (O-2).
- **Re-run the full suite against the promoted `^0.1.0` pins before tagging.** A
  green run against `-dev` pins is not evidence about the shipped artefact.

> `betto_builder_tools` is **not** in the gate — it is a `dev_dependencies` entry
> in all three consumers and never reaches a consumer's resolution.

### Smaller independents (Group E)

Each is small and independently shippable; no plan needed unless one grows.

- **C-1** — document, or warn, when `dbDir` is outside the Windows user profile.
  Largely superseded by the controlled-location secret store in the Group A plan.
- **C-2** — `chmod 600` the REPL history file.
- **L-1** — `kmdb help <unknown>` dies with an unhandled exception and exit 255.
- **S-5** — replace the hand-rolled SHA-256/CRC32C with `package:crypto`. The
  implementation is **correct** (verified against NIST vectors) but contradicts
  the project's own stance in `plan_vault_gc_failsafe.md`, and is unverified on
  web where `int` semantics differ.
- **S-8** — size, depth, and time bounds on the PDF/HTML/Markdown extractors.
- **F-1** — move native allocations inside the `try` in `betto_zstd.compress`
  and `betto_icu`.

## Deferred to proposals

Design work that is real but must not block `0.1.0`:

- **[betto_secret_store](../proposals/betto_secret_store.md)** — a shared
  Bettongia package providing platform keychain **and** directory-based secret
  storage behind one interface. The Group A plan ships a KMDB-local
  implementation; this proposal is the path to extracting it.
- **[Per-device identity (T3)](../proposals/device_identity.md)** — per-device
  asymmetric keys, enrollment, and revocation, closing the malicious-peer gap.
